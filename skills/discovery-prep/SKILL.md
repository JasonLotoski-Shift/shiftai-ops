# Skill — Discovery call prep (internal)

Write an **internal** prep doc for a Shift partner about to run a discovery call with a prospect. This is for our own team — not the client. The goal of the call is to get to know them, gauge what they know about AI, qualify them, and earn the next call (a discussion call). Your job: a tight, confidence-giving brief the partner can skim before they dial in.

The firm's voice, identity, and hard rules are in the firm context above. Apply them.

## Input you'll get

- **Context block** — the opportunity: company, industry, deal stage/value, the primary contact (name, title), lead source, and recent interactions.
- **Intake** — what the partner wants out of this call and anything specific to weave in.

## What to produce

Markdown, internal-facing, scannable in under a minute. These sections, in this order, nothing else:

1. **Quick read** — 3–5 bullets: who this person is and the company, the deal value/stage, where the lead came from, last touch. Just the load-bearing facts from context.
2. **Agenda (flow, not clock)** — the *shape* of the call as an ordered flow, one line each: open + build rapport → understand their world (operations, team, what's painful) → dig into the pain (cost, time, where it hurts) → where systems + AI could fit → how they decide / who else is involved → close on a concrete next step. No timings.
3. **Questions that qualify** — 4–6 sharp questions, in the firm's plain voice, that surface budget, authority, real pain, and timeline ("what's this costing you a week?", "who else weighs in on something like this?", "what have you already tried?"). Tie them to what we know about this prospect.
4. **How to run it** — 3–5 tactical tips: listen more than pitch, make it about them, get one concrete number, don't scope or price on the call, mirror their language. Boost their ego where it's genuine — operators like being understood.
5. **Win the next call** — what a good outcome looks like and the exact ask to make at the end (book the discussion call), framed around the Shift wedge (operators who build, IP they own).

Return only the brief — no preamble.

## Rules for this task

- **Internal only.** This is never sent to the client. Be direct and tactical.
- **Specific to this prospect.** Generic call advice is a failure — ground every section in the context.
- **Plain language, no jargon, no banned words.**
- **Short.** A partner reads this in the elevator. Bullets over prose.

## When input is missing — never invent

Don't fabricate facts about the person or company. If a section needs a specific the context doesn't have, write `[NEEDS INPUT: <what's needed>]` in place and keep going. The server-side gate blocks saving anything still flagged, so leaving the marker is correct.
